Jackfruit is a large tropical fruit that originates from the rainforests of South and Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is scientifically known as Artocarpus heterophyllus. The jackfruit tree can grow up to 20-80 feet in height and produces the largest tree-borne fruit in the world. The fruit itself is quite distinctive in appearance and can be quite enormous, weighing anywhere from 10 to 100 pounds, with some exceptionally large fruits reaching up to 200 pounds. Each aril contains a seed, which can also be eaten or used for propagation. Jackfruit is appreciated not only for its size but also for its unique flavor and texture.
